📜 📜 History

The concept of energy evolved slowly through the 19th century as physicists realised that heat, motion, electricity, and chemical reactions were all different forms of the same thing.

James Prescott Joule (1818–1889), an English brewer and physicist, conducted meticulous experiments showing that mechanical work could be converted into heat. His famous experiment involved a falling weight turning a paddle wheel in water — the friction heated the water. This established the mechanical equivalent of heat.

Key figures:
- Galileo & Newton — understood kinetic energy ()
- Thomas Young (1807) — first used the word "energy" in the modern scientific sense
- Joule (1840s) — established the relationship between work and heat
- Hermann von Helmholtz (1847) — formalised the Law of Conservation of Energy

Myth vs Fact: Perpetual motion machines (devices that produce more energy than they consume) are impossible. The First Law of Thermodynamics (Conservation of Energy) means energy cannot be created or destroyed — only transformed. Every proposed perpetual motion machine either stops or violates this law.

🌍 🌍 Nature & Applications

  • 🔋 Batteries: Convert chemical energy → electrical energy
  • ☀️ Solar panels: Convert light energy → electrical energy
  • 🧲 Generators: Convert kinetic energy → electrical energy
  • 🍔 Food: Chemical energy in food → thermal energy (warmth) + kinetic energy (movement)
  • 🚗 Electric cars: Battery chemical energy → electrical → kinetic (motion)
  • 🌊 Hydroelectric dams: Gravitational potential energy → kinetic → electrical
  • 🪁 Wind turbines: Kinetic energy of wind → kinetic energy of blades → electrical energy

⚛️ ⚛️ Theory

Energy

Energy is the capacity to do work. SI unit: joule (J).

Principle of Conservation of Energy:

Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It can only be transferred from one form to another.

Forms of Energy

| Gravitational potential | or | Energy due to height | Raised weight, water behind a dam |
