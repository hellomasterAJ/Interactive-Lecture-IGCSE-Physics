# Newton's First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia)

## 📖 Etymology

The Latin name in Newton's *Principia* is **"Lex I"** — the First Law. Newton referred to it as the **"Lex Inertiæ"** (Law of Inertia), though this specific name was popularised by later commentators.

**Inertia** comes from Latin *iners* ("inactive, sluggish") — the same root gives us "inert" (chemically inactive). Newton defined inertia as the inherent property of matter to resist changes in its state of motion.

---

## 📜 Historical Journey to the First Law

The First Law was not discovered by Newton alone — it was the culmination of a 2,000-year struggle against Aristotelian physics:

| Thinker | View on Motion |
|---------|---------------|
| **Aristotle** (350 BCE) | Motion requires continuous force. Without force, objects stop immediately |
| **John Philoponus** (6th C.) | Impetus theory — a moving object carries an internal force that keeps it going |
| **Jean Buridan** (14th C.) | Impetus is proportional to speed and mass; in a vacuum, it would last forever |
| **Galileo** (1590–1638) | **Principle of inertia**: a ball rolling on a horizontal surface continues forever |
| **Descartes** (1644) | *"Each thing, as far as it can, always continues in the same state"* — close but still imprecise |
| **Newton** (1687) | The precise, rigorous statement that defines **inertial reference frames** |

Newton's genius was not discovering that objects resist change — it was making this the **first axiom** from which all of mechanics follows, and recognising that it **defines the very framework** within which the other laws operate.

## 📐 The Law

### Newton's Original (Latin, 1687)

> *"Corpus omne perseverare in statu suo quiescendi vel movendi uniformiter in directum, nisi quatenus a viribus impressis cogitur statum illum mutare."*

### Modern Statement

> **An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at constant velocity in a straight line, unless acted upon by a net external force.**

$$ \boxed{\sum \vec{F} = 0 \iff \vec{v} = \text{constant}} $$

### Key Meanings

| Concept | What It Means |
|---------|--------------|
| **Net external force = 0** | All forces acting on the object cancel out |
| **Constant velocity** | Both speed AND direction are constant (including $v = 0$) |
| **Inertia** | The resistance to changing velocity — proportional to mass |
| **Inertial frame** | A reference frame in which the First Law holds (non-accelerating) |

### The First Law Defines Inertial Frames

This is the **deepest purpose** of the First Law. It tells us which reference frames are valid for applying physics:

- **Inertial frame**: Newton's Laws hold (e.g., a stationary lab, a smoothly moving train)
- **Non-inertial frame**: Newton's Laws appear violated (e.g., a spinning merry-go-round, a braking car) — we need **fictitious forces** (centrifugal, Coriolis) to explain motion

> 💡 **IGCSE context**: At IGCSE, students learn the First Law as "an object at rest stays at rest unless a force acts on it." The concept of inertial frames is introduced at A-Level.

## 🧪 Key Experiment — The Inertia Demonstration

**The Tablecloth Trick:** A tablecloth is quickly pulled from under dishes. The dishes remain in place due to **inertia of rest** — the force of the cloth is applied too briefly to overcome the dishes' inertia.

**The Card and Coin:** A coin is placed on a card on top of a glass. Flick the card away horizontally — the coin drops **straight down** into the glass. The coin was at rest and remained at rest (vertically) until the card left, then gravity acted.

## 🌍 Everyday Examples

| Situation | First Law in Action |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 🚗 **Sudden braking** | Your body lurches forward — it was moving with the car and continues moving forward (inertia of motion) |
| 🏃 **Tripping** | Your feet stop but your upper body keeps moving forward — you fall forward |
| 🚌 **Bus accelerating** | Your body lurches backward — it was at rest relative to the ground and tends to stay at rest |
| 🪰 **Dusting a rug** | Whacking a rug with a stick makes the dust fly off — the rug moves but the dust stays (inertia of rest) |
| 🛰️ **Spacecraft coasting** | In deep space (no friction, no gravity from nearby bodies), a spacecraft keeps moving at constant velocity forever with no thrust |

## 💡 Common Misconceptions

| Misconception | Truth |
|--------------|-------|
| "The First Law is just the Second Law with $a=0$" | ❌ The First Law **defines inertial frames** — it is a prerequisite for applying the Second Law, not a special case |
| "An object in motion must have a force on it to keep moving" | ❌ This is Aristotle's error. Newton says once moving, it stays moving with **zero net force** |
| "Inertia is a force that keeps things moving" | ❌ Inertia is **not a force** — it is a property of matter, the resistance to *change* in motion |
