🧱 Mass is the amount of matter in an object, measured in kilograms (kg) with a balance. It is a property of the object itself — it never changes, no matter which planet you are on.
🪝 Weight is the force of gravity pulling on a mass, measured in newtons (N) with a spring balance (newtonmeter). It depends on gravity: W = m × g, so it changes from planet to planet.
