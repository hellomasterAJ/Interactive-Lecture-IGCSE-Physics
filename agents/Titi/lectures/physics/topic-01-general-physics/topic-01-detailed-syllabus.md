## 1.1 Physical Quantities and Measurement Techniques

### 1.1.1 Scalars & Vectors
- **Scalar:** magnitude only (mass, time, speed, distance, energy)
- **Vector:** magnitude + direction (force, velocity, acceleration, momentum, weight)
- Representation: arrow diagram (length = magnitude, arrowhead = direction)

### 1.1.2 Length Measurement
| Instrument | Precision | Measures |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Metre ruler | ±1 mm | Lengths > 10 cm |
| Vernier caliper | ±0.1 mm | Small lengths, diameter, depth |
| Micrometer screw gauge | ±0.01 mm | Thin wires, small spheres |

- Parallax error — eye must be perpendicular to scale
- Zero error — reading when jaws closed (subtract or add)

### 1.1.3 Time Measurement
- Stopwatch / digital timer — ±0.01 s (manual), ±0.001 s (light gate)
- Light gates + data logger → accurate speed/acceleration
- Pendulum: T = 2π√(l/g)

### 1.1.4 Volume & Area
- Regular solid: V = l × w × h (ruler)
- Irregular solid: displacement method (Eureka can / measuring cylinder)
- Liquid: measuring cylinder (read meniscus at eye level)
- Area: counting squares on graph paper, or formula

## 1.2 Motion

### 1.2.1 Speed, Velocity & Acceleration
- **Speed (scalar):** v = d/t (m/s or km/h)
- **Velocity (vector):** v = s/t (displacement/time)
- **Acceleration (vector):** a = (v−u)/t (m/s²)

### 1.2.2 Distance-Time Graphs
| Slope | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Horizontal | Stationary |
| Straight diagonal | Constant speed |
| Curved (steeper) | Accelerating |
| Slope gradient | = speed |

### 1.2.3 Speed-Time / Velocity-Time Graphs
| Slope | Area under | Meaning |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Gradient = acceleration | Area = distance travelled | — |
| Horizontal line | — | Constant velocity |
| Positive slope | — | Acceleration |
| Negative slope | — | Deceleration |
| Curve | — | Changing acceleration (jerk) |

### 1.2.4 Equations of Motion (SUVAT)
For uniform acceleration only:
```
v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as
```
where: u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = acceleration, t = time, s = displacement

### 1.2.5 Free Fall
- Near Earth: g ≈ 9.8 m/s² (≈10 N/kg for calculations)
- All objects fall at same rate in vacuum (no air resistance)
- Terminal velocity: air resistance = weight → net force = 0 → constant velocity
- Parachute: larger surface area → lower terminal velocity

### 1.2.6 Thinking, Braking & Stopping Distance
- **Stopping distance = Thinking distance + Braking distance**
- Thinking distance ↑ with speed, tiredness, alcohol, drugs, distractions
- Braking distance ↑ with speed (×4 when speed ×2), wet/icy roads, worn tyres/brakes

## 1.3 Mass and Weight

### 1.3.1 Definitions
| Quantity | Definition | Unit | Instrument |
|----------|-----------|------|------------|
| **Mass** | Amount of matter | kg (scalar) | Electronic balance / triple beam balance |
| **Weight** | Force of gravity | N (vector) | Spring balance / newtonmeter |

### 1.3.2 Relationship
- **W = mg** (where g = gravitational field strength ≈ 9.8 N/kg on Earth)
- Moon: g ≈ 1.6 N/kg → weight = 1/6 of Earth; mass unchanged
- Weight changes with location; mass is constant

### 1.3.3 Gravitational Field Strength
- g = 9.8 N/kg on Earth surface (≈ 10 N/kg for rough calculations)
- g decreases with altitude; varies slightly across Earth's surface
- g = GM/r² (Newton's Law of Gravitation — Supplement)

## 1.4 Density

### 1.4.1 Definition & Formula
- **ρ = m/V** (kg/m³ or g/cm³)
- 1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³
- Density of water = 1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³

### 1.4.2 Measuring Density
| Object | Method |
|--------|--------|
| Regular solid | m (balance) + V = l×w×h (ruler) |
| Irregular solid | m + displacement (Eureka can) |
| Liquid | m of empty cylinder → m of cylinder + liquid → V from cylinder |

### 1.4.3 Floating & Sinking
- Object floats if ρ_object < ρ_fluid
- Object sinks if ρ_object > ρ_fluid
- Object is suspended if ρ_object = ρ_fluid
- Hydrometer: measures density of liquids (floats higher in denser liquid)

## 1.5 Forces

### 1.5.1 Types of Forces
| Force | Direction | Contact? |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Weight (gravity) | Towards Earth centre | Non-contact |
| Friction | Opposite to motion | Contact |
| Air resistance (drag) | Opposite to motion | Contact |
| Tension | Along string/rope | Contact |
| Normal reaction | Perpendicular to surface | Contact |
| Thrust | Direction of motion | Contact |
| Upthrust (buoyancy) | Upwards | Contact |
| Magnetic | Direction of field | Non-contact |
| Electrostatic | Towards opposite charge | Non-contact |

### 1.5.2 Hooke's Law
- **F = kx** (where k = spring constant in N/m, x = extension in m)
- Extension ∝ force (up to limit of proportionality)
- Elastic limit: beyond this point, spring does not return to original length
- Spring constant k: stiffness of spring (higher k = stiffer)
- Springs in series: 1/k_total = 1/k₁ + 1/k₂
- Springs in parallel: k_total = k₁ + k₂

### 1.5.3 Newton's Laws of Motion
| Law | Statement | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| **1st Law (Inertia)** | Object at rest stays at rest, moving stays moving, unless acted by external force | Book on table stays still; car crash stops suddenly |
| **2nd Law** | F = ma (force = mass × acceleration) | Pushing a trolley harder → accelerates more |
| **3rd Law** | Every action has equal and opposite reaction | Rocket pushes gas down → gas pushes rocket up |

### 1.5.4 Friction
- Opposes motion; produces heat; wears surfaces
- Useful: brakes, walking, gripping, tyres on road
- Harmful: engine wear, heat loss, reduced efficiency
- Reducing friction: lubricants, ball bearings, streamlining, air cushion

### 1.5.5 Centre of Gravity (CoG)
- Point where entire weight of object appears to act
- CoG position affects stability:
  - Low CoG + wide base → stable
  - High CoG + narrow base → unstable
- Finding CoG: plumb line method (irregular lamina)

### 1.5.6 Moments (Turning Effect)
- **Moment = Force × Perpendicular distance from pivot** (N m)
- **Principle of Moments:** Sum of clockwise moments = Sum of anticlockwise moments (for equilibrium)
- Levers: effort × effort arm = load × load arm
- Examples: seesaw, wrench, wheelbarrow, crowbar

### 1.5.7 Equilibrium
- Object is in equilibrium when:
  - Net force = 0 (translational equilibrium)
  - Net moment = 0 (rotational equilibrium)
- CoG must be above base of support for stable equilibrium

## 1.6 Momentum (Supplement Only)

### 1.6.1 Definition
- **p = mv** (kg m/s)
- Vector quantity (direction = direction of velocity)

### 1.6.2 Conservation of Momentum
- **Total momentum before = Total momentum after** (in a closed system)
- m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂

### 1.6.3 Collisions
| Type | KE conserved? | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Elastic | ✅ Yes | Billiard balls, gas molecules |
| Inelastic | ❌ No (KE → heat/sound) | Car crash, train coupling |

### 1.6.4 Impulse
- **Impulse = Force × Time = Change in momentum** (F·Δt = Δp)
- Crumple zones, airbags, seatbelts: ↑Δt → ↓F (same impulse = same Δp)

## 1.7 Energy, Work and Power

### 1.7.1 Energy Stores & Transfers
| Store | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Kinetic | Movement energy | Moving car, wind |
| Gravitational Potential | Height above ground | Raised weight, water in dam |
| Elastic | Stretched/compressed | Rubber band, spring |
| Chemical | Bonds in substances | Food, battery, fuel |
| Nuclear | Nucleus reactions | Sun, nuclear power |
| Thermal (Internal) | Heat energy | Hot object |
| Light | Electromagnetic radiation | Sun, lamp |
| Sound | Vibrations in medium | Loudspeaker |
| Electrical | Movement of charges | Power lines |

**Conservation of Energy:** Energy cannot be created or destroyed; only transferred from one store to another.

### 1.7.2 Kinetic Energy
- **KE = ½mv²** (J)
- KE ∝ v² → doubling speed quadruples KE

### 1.7.3 Gravitational Potential Energy
- **GPE = mgh** (J)
- Reference level (h=0) is arbitrary; only change in GPE matters

### 1.7.4 Work
- **W = Fd** (J) where d = distance moved in direction of force
- Work done = energy transferred
- 1 Joule = work done when 1 N force moves 1 m

### 1.7.5 Power
- **P = W/t = E/t** (W)
- 1 Watt = 1 Joule per second
- Horsepower: 1 hp ≈ 746 W

### 1.7.6 Efficiency
- **Efficiency = (Useful output energy / Total input energy) × 100%**
- No machine is 100% efficient (friction/heat loss)
- Sankey diagram: visual representation of energy transfers (width ∝ energy)

### 1.7.7 Energy Resources
| Type | Renewable? | Pros | Cons |
|------|-----------|------|------|
| Fossil fuels (coal/oil/gas) | ❌ | Reliable, high energy density | CO₂, pollution, finite |
| Nuclear | ❌ (fuel finite) | Low CO₂, high output | Radioactive waste, disaster risk |
| Solar | ✅ | Free, clean | Intermittent, needs space |
| Wind | ✅ | Free, clean | Intermittent, noisy, visual |
| Hydroelectric | ✅ | Reliable, quick response | Dam floods habitats |
| Tidal | ✅ | Predictable | Expensive, affects ecosystems |
| Geothermal | ✅ | Constant | Location-specific |
| Wave | ✅ | Clean | Unreliable, expensive |
| Biomass | ✅ | Waste-to-energy | Land use, air pollution |

## 1.8 Pressure

### 1.8.1 Definition
- **P = F/A** (Pa or N/m²)
- 1 Pa = 1 N/m²

### 1.8.2 Pressure in Solids
- Smaller area → greater pressure (same force)
- Examples: knife blade (small area → cuts), snow shoes (large area → don't sink)

### 1.8.3 Pressure in Liquids
- **P = ρgh** (density × gravitational field × depth)
- Pressure depends on: density of liquid, depth — **NOT** on shape of container
- Pressure acts equally in all directions at a given depth
- **Hydraulic Systems (Pascal's Principle):** Pressure transmitted equally throughout fluid
  - F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂ (car brake, hydraulic lift — force multiplier)

### 1.8.4 Atmospheric Pressure
- ≈ 101,300 Pa (1 atm) at sea level
- Decreases with altitude (less air above)
- **Barometer:** measures atmospheric pressure (mercury barometer, aneroid barometer)
- **Manometer:** measures gas pressure (U-tube with liquid)

### 1.8.5 Manometer Basics
- Gas pressure = Atmospheric pressure + ρgh (if gas side higher)
- Gas pressure = Atmospheric pressure − ρgh (if open side higher)
