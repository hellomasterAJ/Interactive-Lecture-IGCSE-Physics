1.1.2 Length Measurement
IGCSE Physics 0625
📜 The Story

Long before digital callipers, people used their own bodies to measure length — the cubit (elbow to fingertip), the foot, and the inch (thumb width). The problem? Everyone's body is different!

In 1799, France introduced the metre — defined as one ten-millionth of the distance from the North Pole to the Equator. A platinum bar was created: the Metre des Archives. This was the world's first standard length.

— Today the metre is defined by the speed of light: 1 m = the distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 s

📖 How to Read a Micrometer Screw Gauge
💡 How to read: Main scale reading + Thimble scale reading

In this example: Main scale = 4.5 mm (4 mm + 0.5 mm half-mark), Thimble = 22 (22 × 0.01 = 0.22 mm), Total = 4.72 mm

⚠️ Common Mistake
❌ Taking a measurement without checking the zero error.
✅ Always close the calliper/micrometer first and record the zero reading. If it reads +0.02 mm when closed, subtract 0.02 mm from every measurement.
❌ Reading the scale from an angle.
✅ Your eye must be directly perpendicular (straight on) to the scale to avoid parallax error.
📝 Worked Example

A student measures the diameter of a wire with a micrometer.

Given:

• Main scale reading = 2.5 mm
• Thimble scale = 38
• Zero error = −0.02 mm

Solution:

Step 1: Raw reading = 2.5 + (38 × 0.01) = 2.5 + 0.38 = 2.88 mm
Step 2: Corrected reading = 2.88 − (−0.02) = 2.88 + 0.02 = 2.90 mm
Diameter = 2.90 mm
