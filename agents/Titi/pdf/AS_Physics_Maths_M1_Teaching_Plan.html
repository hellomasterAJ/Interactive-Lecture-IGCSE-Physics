AS Physics & Mathematics (Mechanics 1) - Homeschool Teaching Plan

10-month online course · 1.5-hour sessions · 2x/week per subject · Target sitting: Summer (May/June) 2027

1. Course Overview

This plan covers the two International Advanced Subsidiary (IAS) units of Edexcel International A Level Physics (Units 1, 2 and 3) and the Mechanics 1 (M1) unit of Edexcel International A Level Mathematics, delivered one-to-one online in 1.5-hour sessions, two sessions per week per subject (4 sessions/week total), over roughly 43 weeks from mid-July 2026 to early May 2027, ending with the Summer 2027 examination series.

2. Physics - Unit Summary

Note: at least 40% of the marks across all Physics papers target mathematics skills at Level 2 or above (graphs, trigonometry, algebraic rearrangement, standard form) - this is reinforced throughout by the parallel Maths M1 sessions.

3. Mathematics M1 - Unit Summary

5. Physics Core Practicals Checklist (Units 1 & 2)

Since sessions are online, practicals can be delivered via video demonstration + data analysis, remote-lab simulations (e.g. PhET, Multisim, IsaacPhysics), or by asking the student to run a simplified version at home under guidance - Unit 3 (WPH13) tests understanding of method, uncertainty and analysis rather than requiring lab attendance.

6. Recommended Resources

Textbooks: Pearson Edexcel International A Level Physics Student Book 1 (Units 1-3); Pearson Edexcel International A Level Mathematics Mechanics 1 Student Book

Official specifications & past papers: qualifications.pearson.com (search “International Advanced Level Physics” / “Mathematics” specifications and past papers)

Past papers & mark schemes: savemyexams.com, physicsandmathstutor.com (PMT) - both index Edexcel IAL Physics and M1 papers by topic

Formula sheet & data sheet: provided by Pearson in the exam - practise using it from the start so the student is fluent by the mocks

Practical/virtual lab support: PhET Interactive Simulations, IsaacPhysics.org for extra problem sets and practical-skills style questions

7. Planning Notes & Flexibility

This is a working scaffold, not a rigid script - use the Week 1 diagnostic and ongoing homework performance to compress or stretch individual topics.

Because the student's starting level hasn't been assessed yet, Weeks 2-5 build in natural checkpoints (kinematics, then dynamics) where pace can be adjusted before committing to the rest of the schedule.

The plan assumes M1 is being taught as a standalone applied unit; if Pure Mathematics 1 (P1) also needs to be covered for a full AS Maths award, a separate P1 track (or additional weekly sessions) will need to be added.

Two full mock checkpoints (Weeks 20 and 41) are built in deliberately - timed, full-paper conditions matter as much as content coverage for IAL-style exams.

Holiday buffer (Weeks 22-25) can absorb schedule slippage; if the course is running ahead, use it for extra past-paper practice instead.
