1.2 Motion
Speed, Velocity & Acceleration
Speed is a scalar quantity — it has magnitude only. Velocity is a vector — it has both magnitude and direction.
v = s / t   |   a = (v − u) / t
💡 Key Point: An object moving at constant speed in a circle has changing velocity (direction changes) — therefore it is accelerating!
⚠️ Common Mistake: Students think "no acceleration" means "no motion." Actually, constant velocity = zero acceleration, even if speed is high.
📝 Worked Example: A car accelerates from 10 m/s to 25 m/s in 5 s. Find acceleration.
a = (25 − 10) / 5 = 3 m/s²
